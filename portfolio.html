Follow Medication Instructions:

Always take medications exactly as prescribed by your healthcare provider or as directed on the label. If you have any questions about your medications, don't hesitate to ask your pharmacist.

Check Expiry Dates:

Before taking any medication, check the expiration date. Expired medications may not be as effective and could potentially be harmful.

Traveling with Medications:

If you're traveling, plan ahead and make sure you have enough medication for the duration of your trip. Be aware of any special storage requirements.

Ask Questions:

If you're unsure about anything related to your medications, ask your pharmacist. They can provide information on proper usage, potential side effects, and interactions with other drugs.

Keep a Medication List:

Maintain an up-to-date list of all your medications, including prescription and over-the-counter drugs, as well as any supplements. Share this list with your healthcare providers and pharmacist.

Allergies:

Make sure your pharmacist is aware of any drug allergies you have to avoid potential allergic reactions. By fostering open communication with your pharmacist regarding your drug allergies, you contribute to a collaborative and informed healthcare approach.
